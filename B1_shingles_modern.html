Tracking the shingles shot surge across the United States

We mapped how shingles vaccination coverage rebounded between 2020 and 2023, which states are racing ahead, and where older adults are still falling behind. The briefing gives health agencies decision-ready boards and talking points for rapid response.

Published August 14, 2025
Read time 7 minutes
Series Adult Vaccination Observatory
Studio leads Bhairavi Jayaraman & Poli Plot Labs

Inside this briefing

  • Six-panel uptake dashboard comparing 2020 vs. 2023 state performance.
  • Equity spotlight on rural South counties and Native communities.
  • Policy levers and messaging frames sustaining the rebound.

Pair it with the activation kit to brief partners, commissioners, or newsroom stakeholders in under ten minutes.

Why it matters

Shingles, or herpes zoster, will touch roughly one in three Americans, and the pain can linger for months in older adults. The CDC recommends the two-dose Shingrix series for people aged 50 and older, making shingles uptake a reliable pulse check on whether preventive care is reaching seniors.

The 2025 landscape adds urgency: the 65+ population is set to double by 2040 while vaccine misinformation hardens. Insurers and state health departments are already reassessing coverage strategies, so evidence on who is falling behind and what messaging works is mission critical.

What we found

  • Coverage climbed everywhere. Every state posted gains between 2020 and 2023, with Vermont, North Dakota, and South Dakota topping 60 percent of adults 60+ completing the series.
  • Southern states are lagging. Mississippi, Alabama, and Arkansas remain under 40 percent, signaling persistent gaps in rural access, cold chain capacity, and trusted messengers.
  • Equity gaps are narrowing, but fragile. Counties with strong pharmacist outreach and aging-service networks saw the fastest rebounds; those wins are vulnerable if statewide funding cycles slip.

Signals to watch

  • Pharmacy-based outreach. States piloting pharmacist counseling and door-to-door scheduling are building durable trust with older adults.
  • Benefit design shifts. Insurers are testing zero-dollar shingles shots; sunset clauses or benefit carve-outs could erase the recent progress.
  • Emerging misinformation. Sentiment tracking shows that vaccine skepticism spikes when leadership narratives change. Counter-messaging toolkits should launch alongside policy announcements.

Data & methodology

The analysis draws on CDC BRFSS state-level adult vaccination coverage for shingles between 2020 and 2023. We filtered to respondents aged 60 and older, averaged coverage within each state-year, and flagged jurisdictions missing 2023 values. The briefing is descriptive—built to anchor stakeholder conversations and spotlight outliers for deeper qualitative work.
